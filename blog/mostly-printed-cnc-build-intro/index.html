Mostly Printed CNC Build - Intro

Tuesday, Feb 6, 2018
Blog

Over the next month or two, I will build the Mostly Printed CNC (MPCNC), as found on the website v1engineering.com. It is a CNC platform that can be assembled out of inexpensive electronics and hardware, EMT conduit, and 3d printed components. Here is a link to the project specifications and build instructions.

This machine can be much more than a typical milling machine: pen plotters, needle cutters, drag knifes, even laser cutting and engraving! The build size can be any length or width, but anything longer than 3.5 feet would need some supports along the conduit rails. I intend to start with a milling tool and a drag knife, but hope to expand once I get the hang of the system. As for build size, I am shooting for 18"x18" to 24"x24." Eventually, I would like to build/expand to a tool that can work on a full sheet of plywood. That may be a job for the LowriderCNC!

I have about 100 hours of print time ahead of me to get the parts done. As I do that, I will slowly aquire the other necessary components, and try to carve out a space to build this thing. Be looking for an update in the next week on my progress.
